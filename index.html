Hi — I’m Lichao.

I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) at the University of Bristol, working in hardware security and AI security. My research focuses on AI-augmented physical and micro-architectural hardware security, as well as the security and safety of AI systems.

I previously held postdoctoral positions in leading security groups in Europe. I was a postdoctoral researcher in the System Security Lab at TU Darmstadt, working with Prof. Ahmad-Reza Sadeghi, and before that a postdoctoral fellow at Radboud University, led by Prof. Joan Daemen and Prof. Stjepan Picek. I obtained my Ph.D. from TU Delft in 2023, supervised by Prof. Inald Lagendijk and Prof. Stjepan Picek.

I have published in top-tier security and cryptography venues including USENIX Security, NDSS, ASIACRYPT, TCHES, and TDSC, and received the Informatics Best PhD Dissertation Award (2023) and an NDSS Distinguished Paper Award (2026). Several of my works have been referenced in AIS 46 guidance by the German Federal Office for Information Security (BSI).

Alongside academia, I worked as a Principal Security Evaluator at SGS Brightsight, the Netherlands (2017–2024), leading several CC EAL5+ security evaluations for globally leading IC designers and running R&D initiatives with universities worldwide.

I am looking for motivated PhD students and postdoctoral researchers — see open positions.

  • EPSRC Fellowships — anywhere
